Be Intentional About Company CULTURE


Culture is what give an organization its power. It surrounds us and permeates everything we do, binding us together for good or ill. Whether we acknowledge it or not, there is always power in our company's culture. Always.

When we are intentional about driving positive culture...
We can harness that power to increase employee engagement and productivity. Additionally, culture and brand go hand in hand, so a strong positive culture can go a long way toward positioning your company as a front-runner in your industry.

If we ignore culture, we do so at our own peril.
Culture exists regardless of our attentiveness. When ignored, it can become toxic and destructive, causing uncertainty and fear among employees. That fear can lead to anger, hate, and miserable working conditions.

Don't go to the dark side.
Lay a solid cultural foundation for productivity and success.




Implement Effective COMMUNICATION


Gone are the days when employees were hired, put to work, and evaluated annually The workforce is evolving, and thrives in an environment of frequent feedback and recognition. Without such an environment, not only do we have a failure to communicate, we have an inability to develop and retain engaged talent.

Gather candid employee feedback.
An employee who feels understood plays with a real cool hand. Employees often have great ideas, pertinent feedback, or serious concerns that need to be recognized. Without a well-established and safe channel for feedback, employees will either suffer in silence or eventually express their feedback in unproductive ways.

Employee Engagement

Be proactive.
If you are hearing about discontent through the grapevine, the problem is already out of hand. Approach employees early and often to determine what small measures can create huge returns.

Recognize excellence in every employee. A good manager recognizes that everyone has opportunities for improvement. Great managers can identify moments of greatness in every employee, even if they are not a natural born world-shaker. Shining a spotlight on those moments effectively encourages our better angels to overcome our lesser tendencies.

When we hold ourselves and others equally accountable for success, then we can truly overcome all of the obstacles that threaten to destroy our success and prosperity.

Commit to EMPLOYEE DEVELOPMENT


You have got to believe. Operating from a growth mindset, we realize that employees at any level can grow, and can improve. This frame of mind allows us to have a realistic expectation that any employee, managed properly, can excel in their profession.

Career Planning...
Every employee likes to know that there are bigger things on the horizon. In most cases, it is also advantageous to the company to have just-in-time talent available to fill vacant roles. Plan and implement your strategy to avoid the difficulties associated with getting new or unqualified talent up to speed.

Individualized Engagement
Employee development programs on a large scale are laudable, but engaging with every employee identically is like trying to eat fifty hard boiled eggs in one sitting. You are going to lose more than a few.
